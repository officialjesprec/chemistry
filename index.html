Acids — 10 Examples

An acid donates H⁺ ions in solution, has pH < 7, turns blue litmus red, and reacts with metals to produce hydrogen gas.

⚡ Strong Acids

  • 1.
    Hydrochloric Acid — HCl
    Found in stomach; very corrosive
  • 2.
    Sulphuric Acid — H₂SO₄
    Used in car batteries; industrial chemical
  • 3.
    Nitric Acid — HNO₃
    Used in making fertilizers & explosives
  • 4.
    Hydrobromic Acid — HBr
    Strong acid, highly corrosive
  • 5.
    Perchloric Acid — HClO₄
    Strongest common acid

🌿 Weak / Organic Acids

  • 6.
    Ethanoic Acid — CH₃COOH
    Acetic acid; found in vinegar
  • 7.
    Citric Acid — C₆H₈O₇
    Found in citrus fruits like orange & lemon
  • 8.
    Carbonic Acid — H₂CO₃
    In fizzy drinks (CO₂ + H₂O)
  • 9.
    Methanoic Acid — HCOOH
    Formic acid; found in ant stings
